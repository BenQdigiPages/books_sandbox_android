The following topics are covered in this chapter.

  • Coulomb's Law
    • State Coulomb's law in words as the force between two charges is directly proportional to the product of the charges and inversely proportional to the distance between the charges squared
    • Know Coulomb's law can be represented mathematically as F = k q 1 q 2 r 2 F=\frac{kq_1q_2}{r^2} Solve problems using Coulomb's law to calculate the force between two charges
  • (b) Electric Fields
    • Describe an electric field as a region of space in which an electric charge experiences a force. The direction of the electric field at a point is the direction that a positive test charge would move if placed at that point.
    • Draw electric field lines for various configurations of charges (point charges, two point charges, outside a charged hollow sphere, parallel plates)
    • Define the magnitude of the electric field at a point as the force per unit positive charge E = F q E=\frac{F}{q}  where E and F are vectors
    • Solve problems using E = F q E=\frac{F}{q} (no parallel plates)
    • Calculate the electric field strength at a point due to a point charge, using the equation E = k Q r 2 E=\frac{kQ}{r^2}  to determine the contribution to the field due to each charge (Convention: Q represents the charge responsible for the electric field. q represents the charge experiencing the electric field)
    • Determine the resultant electric field for a maximum of two charges
